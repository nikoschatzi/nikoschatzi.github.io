WorldFIP Add-on PCB

Communication cards in cryogenics crates have critical operation. They collect all data from crate's peripheral cards, and they send them through WorldFIP network. There are more than 1200 communication cards installed, most of them in LHC. These cards were designed around 20 years ago, with non-reprogrammable FPGA's. The cost to replace all these cards with new designs would be more than 1 million CHF. For that reasons, any problems can be solved by "adding" extra electronics to these cards, since there is no option to reprogram these FPGA's.

In general, cryogenics electronics are quite reliable. However, some minor non-critical problems do exist. During annual YETS AUG tests at CERN around 20% of these cards do not power on properly. In order to fix that problem, manual interventions and local resets are needed. Apart from this, some very rare corruption errors happened in the past. To solve these, an add-on PCB, containing an FPGA, was designed and installed in less than 100 communication cards. Due to its, high cost it was added only in the most critical cards. If it is installed in all communication cards, the cost would reach more than 100.000 CHF. New addon PCB was designed a few months ago, using only transistors, capacitors, diodes and resistors, achieving very low production cost. Because of it's low cost (total cost 23.000 CHF), it is feasible to be installed in all communication cards.

During YETS 2023, PCB prototypes were added in 19 communication cards. So far, everything works fine. Big production is planned (more than 1300 PCB's) in the next months, and install these PCB's in all communication cards in the next technical stop. Extended laboratory tests were performed, including temperature tests.
